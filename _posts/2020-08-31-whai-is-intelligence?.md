**What is Intelligence really?**

Try to imagine waking up in a room with no doors just a single slider window with a burglary iron attached right before it, there is no other way out -- just that window.

What is your first objective? What are the first sets of thoughts that crosses through your mind? Notice that your first objective initiates Intelligence thus stating that **Intelligence is objective driven.** 

But let's assume it's an animal -- say a rooster -- placed in that same room, but with a door closed but not locked. Which direction do you imagine the rooster will take? If the rooster approaches the door it states the rooster has had an experience with door ways and if it approaches the window it means the rooster has little or no experience with doorways or simply assumes the way out is from the direction of light (assuming its day time) through the window into the room which is a form of understanding shown by the rooster. This states that **Intelligence needs experience.**

Back to you being trapped in that room with no doors. But somewhere around the room there is an hammer and given that's all you have to get out of that room, will it be useful and why? Finding it useful shows a level of understanding of how the burglary iron might have been attached even if you weren't there when it was attached. This is a form of creative Intelligence from the [triarchic theory of intelligence](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/waymaker-psychology/chapter/what-are-intelligence-and-creativity/) has proposed by [Robert Sternberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Sternberg). 

Creative intelligence is marked by inventing or imagining a solution to a problem or situation from an underlying understanding of it basic components, thus inferring **Intelligence is built upon understanding.**

There are actually many definitions of Intelligence. My favourite definition is that of the American researcher [Alexander D. Wissner-Gross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Wissner-Gross)

> F = T ∇ S
>
> Intelligence is a force, F, that acts so as to maximize future freedom of action. It acts to maximize future freedom of action, or keep options open, with some strength T, with the diversity of possible accessible futures, S, up to some future time horizon, τ. In short, intelligence doesn't like to get trapped

Another definition I admire is that of cognitive psychologist Reuven Feuerstein [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reuven_Feuerstein]

> Intelligence is the unique propensity of human beings to change or modify the structure of their cognitive functioning to adapt to the changing demands of a life situation

From the definitions and highlights above, it is reasonable to state that Intelligence is dependent on four (4) major factors:
       
    1. The environment: This is the field, situation or problem an agent is faced with.
    2. The objective or aim: This is the end goal. That is, what the end of Intelligence is suppose to yield.
    3. The experience: This is a result of an agents constant interaction with an environment.
    4. The underlying understanding: This is the comprehension of the underlying component of the environment.
       
Thus it is save to conclude that _Intelligence is the result or outcome of acting on the compilation of thoughts based on the understanding of the environment and objective, with experience._
