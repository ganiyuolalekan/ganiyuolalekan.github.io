The introduction of artificial intelligence was based on the assumption that the processes of thoughts by humans can be [mechanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_artificial_intelligence). This processes of thoughts is a common characteristics assumed by all intelligent agents as a form of reasoning. At this spanish Philosopher [Ramon Liul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramon_Llull) developed several logical machines devoted to the creation of knowledge by logical means, his works could combine basic truths as simple logical operations, produced by the machine by mechanical means, Liull’s work had a great influence on [Gottfried Leibniz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gottfried_Leibniz) and later on in the 17th century, Leibniz, [Thomas Hobbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes) and [Rene Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Descartes) explored the possibility that all rational thought could be made as systematic as algebra or geometry.

In this article I will focus on the **compilation of thoughts** as the process known as **reasoning**, how reasoning are applied in machines and finally how the 6 branches of AI cuts off from the idea of mechanizing thoughts.

In my article [what is intelligence](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/what-intelligence-olalekan-ganiyu/), I highlighted the major factor that initiates intelligence and came to the conclusion that intelligence “is the result or outcome of acting on the compilation of thoughts based on the understanding of the environment and objective, with experience”. 

It was also stated that intelligence is objective driven, intelligence needs experience and intelligence is built upon understanding. The working together of all this principles requires the processing of thoughts – which is the deduction of inferences or interpretations from premises.

<blockquote>
Reasoning is nothing but Reckoning

<cite>Thomas Hobbes</cite>
</blockquote>

Intelligence for all living species is nothing but this compilation of thoughts known as reasoning to yield an objective (an aim). A much practical – and mathematical – form of reasoning is reckoning, where the abstract concepts of reasoning are converted to it’s equivalent mathematical and algorithmic form for machines. This is the form artificial systems uses to simulate intelligence through programming.

<h2>How intelligence is simulated across the branches of AI:</h2>

<h3>Neural Networks (NNs)</h3>

Also referred to as _Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs)_ is a vital branch of AI that focuses on **simulating the activity of neurons in the brains** – preferably humans. 

The neural nets are specialized on pattern recognition and could be used for functional approximation, classification, data processing and a lot [more](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neural_network#Applications).

<h3>Machine Learning (ML)</h3>

The term [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning) pretty much defines itself, thou contextually machine learning deals with the study of computer algorithms which improves automatically through experience. This is an extremely important field in AI as **it simulates the learning process of intelligence itself** – or simple the process of learning. 

ML has three (3) main approaches [supervised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supervised_learning), [unsupervised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unsupervised_learning) or [self-supervised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unsupervised_learning) and [reinforced learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning) and is ever expanding in its [applications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning#Applications).

<h3>Natural Language Processing (NLP)</h3>

[Natural language processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_processing) is a sub-field of linguistics, computer science, information engineering, and artificial intelligence concerned with the interactions between computers and human (natural) languages, **NLP simulates the human ability to understand the everyday language that people use to communicate.** 

This process helps the computer to understand the human language. 

<h3>Robotics</h3>

[Robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robotics) is an interdisciplinary research area, with its aim being to create robots (etymology: form the Slavic word robota meaning slave) to **simulate unique abilities of humans in an area or multiple area of specializations** that may be discomforting, too risky or dangerous for humans to operate in. 

Robotics draws from the achievements of information engineering, computer engineering, mechanical engineering, electronic engineering and others. It’s application includes domestic robots, nano-robots, autonomous robots and [many more](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robotics#Applications).

<h3>Expert Systems</h3>

[Expert systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expert_system) are systems that sorts to emulate or **simulate the knowledge of domain experts**. They were the very first form of artificial intelligence which were able to solve complex problems by reasoning through bodies of knowledge – past expert systems were practically a collection of the knowledge of a domain expert represented in the form of if-then rules. 

The expert systems has several form of application which includes interpretation, prediction, diagnosis and so [many more](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expert_system#Applications).

<h3>Fuzzy Logic</h3>

[Fuzzy logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuzzy_logic) introduced by the proposal of fuzzy set theory by [Lotfi Zadeh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lotfi_A._Zadeh) is used with neural networks to **simulate how a person would make decisions**, only much faster through the aggregation of data and changing into more useful data by forming partial truth or false as fuzzy sets.
